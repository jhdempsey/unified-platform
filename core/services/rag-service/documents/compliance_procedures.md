# Food Safety Compliance Procedures

## Cold Chain Management

### Temperature Monitoring
All temperature-sensitive products must be monitored throughout the supply chain:

- **Frozen Products**: Maintain -10°F to 0°F
- **Refrigerated Products**: Maintain 34°F to 40°F
- **Fresh Produce**: Maintain 34°F to 38°F
- **Dairy Products**: Maintain 36°F to 40°F

### Temperature Deviation Protocol
If temperature exceeds safe ranges:
1. Immediately quarantine affected products
2. Document deviation (time, temperature, duration)
3. Contact Quality Assurance within 30 minutes
4. DO NOT release product without QA approval
5. Conduct risk assessment

## Allergen Management

### Major Allergens Tracked
- Milk and dairy
- Eggs
- Fish (bass, flounder, cod)
- Shellfish (crab, lobster, shrimp)
- Tree nuts (almonds, walnuts, pecans)
- Peanuts
- Wheat
- Soybeans
- Sesame

## Traceability Requirements

### One-Step Forward, One-Step Back
All products must be traceable:
- **Forward**: Know all customers who received each lot
- **Backward**: Know all suppliers who provided ingredients
- **Maximum Time**: Complete trace in 4 hours or less

## Recall Procedures

### Recall Classification
- **Class I**: High risk of serious health consequences
- **Class II**: Temporary adverse effects
- **Class III**: Not likely to cause adverse health effects

### Recall Response Timeline
- **0-2 hours**: Identify affected lots, stop distribution
- **2-4 hours**: Notify customers, begin retrieval
- **24 hours**: Complete customer notification
